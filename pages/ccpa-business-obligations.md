# CCPA Business Obligations

> Businesses subject to the CCPA/CPRA must satisfy notice requirements, maintain compliant privacy policies, impose contractual restrictions on service providers and contractors, and adhere to data minimization principles.

## Key Points
- Privacy policy must disclose categories of PI collected, purposes, third parties, and consumer rights; updated at least every 12 months (s 1798.130)
- Notice must be provided at or before the point of collection (s 1798.100)
- Service providers require written contracts prohibiting sale/sharing and restricting PI use to the direct business relationship (s 1798.140(ag))
- Contractors (CPRA addition) have the same restrictions as service providers plus must allow compliance audits and provide contractual certification (s 1798.140(j))
- Data minimization: collection and use must be reasonably necessary and proportionate to disclosed purposes
- Businesses must respond to verifiable consumer requests within 45 days (extendable to 90)
- A "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" link is required on the homepage (s 1798.135)

## Detail

### Notice Requirements (ss 1798.100, 1798.130)

Businesses must provide consumers with:

1. **Notice at collection:** At or before the point of collection, businesses must inform consumers of the categories of personal information being collected and the purposes for which each category will be used
2. **Privacy policy:** A comprehensive privacy policy that discloses:
   - Categories of personal information collected in the preceding 12 months
   - Categories of sources from which PI is collected
   - Business or commercial purpose for collecting, selling, or sharing PI
   - Categories of third parties with whom PI is shared
   - Specific pieces of PI collected (upon request)
   - A description of each consumer right and how to exercise it
   - Contact information for submitting requests
3. **Update frequency:** The privacy policy must be updated at least every 12 months
4. **Mandatory links:** The homepage must include a "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" link (s 1798.135) and, if applicable, a "Limit the Use of My Sensitive Personal Information" link (s 1798.121)

### Handling Consumer Requests (s 1798.130)

When a business receives a verifiable consumer request:

- **Response time:** 45 calendar days from receipt, extendable by an additional 45 days (90 total) when reasonably necessary, with notice to the consumer
- **Verification:** The business must verify the identity of the requesting consumer
- **Format:** Information must be delivered in a readily usable format; for data portability, in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format
- **Free of charge:** Responses provided at no cost to the consumer
- **Request methods:** At minimum, businesses must provide a toll-free telephone number and, if the business operates a website, a web address for submitting requests

### Service Provider Requirements (s 1798.140(ag))

A service provider is a legal entity that processes personal information on behalf of a business pursuant to a written contract. Requirements:

- **Written contract required** specifying the business purpose for which PI is provided
- **Prohibited actions:** Must not sell or share PI received from the business
- **Use restriction:** Must not retain, use, or disclose PI for any purpose outside the direct business relationship with the contracting business
- **Deletion compliance:** Must comply with consumer deletion requests forwarded by the business
- **No combining:** Must not combine PI received from the business with PI from other sources (except as permitted for the contracted service)

### Contractor Requirements (s 1798.140(j)) -- CPRA Addition

Contractors are subject to the same restrictions as service providers, with additional requirements:

- **Audit rights:** Must contractually agree to allow the business to audit the contractor's compliance
- **Certification:** Must provide a contractual certification that they understand the restrictions and will comply with them
- **Subcontractor flow-down:** Must notify the business if it engages any subcontractor to process PI and impose equivalent restrictions

### Data Minimization (CPRA)

CPRA introduced data minimization principles:

- **Collection limitation:** A business's collection of personal information must be reasonably necessary and proportionate to the purposes for which the PI is collected or processed
- **Purpose limitation:** PI must not be further processed in a manner incompatible with the disclosed purposes
- **Retention limitation:** PI must not be retained longer than reasonably necessary for the disclosed purpose
- **Use limitation:** Use of PI must be reasonably necessary and proportionate to achieve the purposes for which it was collected or processed

### Enforcement Obligations

Businesses should also be aware of:

- **Record-keeping:** Businesses that buy, sell, or share the PI of 10 million or more consumers must compile metrics on consumer requests received, complied with, and denied
- **Risk assessments:** CPPA rulemaking authority (s 1798.185) authorizes regulations requiring risk assessments for processing that presents significant risk to consumer privacy
- **Cybersecurity audits:** CPPA may require annual cybersecurity audits for businesses whose processing presents significant risk
