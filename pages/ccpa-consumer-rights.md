# CCPA Consumer Rights

> The CCPA/CPRA grants California consumers six distinct privacy rights: the right to know, delete, correct, opt-out of sale/sharing, limit use of sensitive personal information, and non-discrimination.

## Key Points
- Six rights total: three from the original CCPA (know, delete, opt-out, non-discrimination) and two added by CPRA (correct, limit sensitive PI use)
- Businesses must respond to verifiable consumer requests within 45 days, extendable to 90 days
- Right to know covers the preceding 12 months (CPRA extends beyond 12 months for PI collected on or after January 1, 2022)
- Opt-out now covers both "sale" and "sharing" for cross-context behavioral advertising
- Minors under 16 require opt-in for sale/sharing; under 13 requires parent/guardian authorization
- Financial incentive programs are permitted under the non-discrimination right if reasonably related to PI value and consumer opts in

## Detail

### 1. Right to Know (ss 1798.100, 1798.110, 1798.115)

Consumers may request that a business disclose:
- Categories of personal information collected (s 1798.110)
- Specific pieces of personal information collected (s 1798.100)
- Categories of sources from which PI was collected (s 1798.110)
- Business or commercial purposes for collecting, selling, or sharing PI (s 1798.110)
- Categories of PI sold or shared and the categories of third parties involved (s 1798.115)
- Categories of third parties to whom PI was disclosed (s 1798.110)

The business must provide this information free of charge, within 45 days of receiving a verifiable consumer request. The response period may be extended by an additional 45 days (total 90 days) when reasonably necessary, with notice to the consumer. Under the original CCPA, the right covered the preceding 12 months. CPRA extends this beyond 12 months for PI collected on or after January 1, 2022.

### 2. Right to Delete (s 1798.105)

Consumers may request deletion of personal information a business has collected from them. Upon receiving a verifiable request, the business must delete the PI and direct its service providers and contractors to do the same.

**Exceptions to deletion** (the business may retain PI if needed to):
- Complete a transaction or provide a requested good/service
- Detect security incidents or protect against malicious, deceptive, or illegal activity
- Exercise free speech or ensure another consumer's right to free speech
- Comply with a legal obligation
- Engage in public or peer-reviewed research in the public interest
- Enable solely internal uses reasonably aligned with consumer expectations

### 3. Right to Correct (s 1798.106) -- CPRA Addition

Consumers may request that a business correct inaccurate personal information it maintains about them. The business must use commercially reasonable efforts to correct the information upon receiving a verifiable request. This right was added by CPRA and became operative January 1, 2023.

### 4. Right to Opt-Out of Sale and Sharing (s 1798.120)

Consumers may direct a business to stop selling or sharing their personal information. Key definitions:

- **Sale** (s 1798.140): Exchanging personal information for monetary or other valuable consideration
- **Sharing** (CPRA addition, s 1798.140): Making personal information available to a third party for cross-context behavioral advertising, regardless of whether monetary exchange occurs

This right is exercised through a mandatory "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" link on the business's homepage (s 1798.135).

**Age-based protections:**
- Consumers aged 16 and older: must opt in before sale/sharing
- Consumers under 16: require affirmative authorization
- Consumers under 13: require parent or guardian authorization

### 5. Right to Limit Use of Sensitive Personal Information (s 1798.121) -- CPRA Addition

Consumers may restrict a business's use and disclosure of sensitive personal information to what is reasonably necessary to perform the requested service or provide the requested goods. Businesses must provide a "Limit the Use of My Sensitive Personal Information" link. See [[ccpa-sensitive-data]] for the full list of sensitive PI categories.

### 6. Right to Non-Discrimination (s 1798.125)

A business cannot discriminate against a consumer for exercising any CCPA right. Prohibited discrimination includes:
- Denying goods or services
- Charging different prices or rates
- Providing a different level or quality of goods or services
- Suggesting the consumer will receive a different price or quality

**Exception:** A business may offer financial incentive programs (e.g., loyalty programs, discounts) related to the collection, sale, or deletion of PI, provided the incentive is reasonably related to the value of the consumer's data and the consumer provides opt-in consent.
