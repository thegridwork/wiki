# AI Act Enforcement Timeline

> The EU AI Act follows a phased enforcement schedule from its entry into force on 1 August 2024 through full application on 2 August 2027, with prohibited practices applying first and product safety AI systems last.

## Key Points
- Entry into force: **1 August 2024**
- Prohibited AI practices (Article 5): **2 February 2025**
- GPAI obligations and governance provisions: **2 August 2025**
- High-risk AI obligations for Annex III systems: **2 August 2026** -- this is the aiact-mcp compliance deadline
- High-risk AI obligations for Annex I product safety systems: **2 August 2027**
- AI regulatory sandboxes must be established: **2 August 2026**
- First evaluation and review by the Commission: **2 August 2029**
- Transitional provisions cover systems already on the market before applicable dates (Article 110)

## Detail

### Complete Deadline Schedule

| Date | What Becomes Applicable | Key Articles |
|------|------------------------|--------------|
| **1 August 2024** | Regulation enters into force (20 days after Official Journal publication on 12 July 2024) | Article 112 |
| **2 February 2025** | Prohibited AI practices banned; AI literacy obligation applies | Articles 4, 5 |
| **2 August 2025** | GPAI model obligations apply; Governance provisions apply (AI Office, Board, Advisory Forum, national authorities) | Articles 51-56 (Title V), Articles 57-63 (Title VI), Articles 64-65 (Title VII) |
| **2 August 2026** | High-risk AI obligations apply for Annex III use-case systems; Transparency obligations apply (Article 50); Penalties framework fully applicable; AI regulatory sandboxes must be established; EU database operational | Articles 6(2), 8-29, 40-49, 50, 57 (sandboxes), 64 (database), 99-101 |
| **2 August 2027** | High-risk AI obligations apply for Annex I product safety systems (safety components requiring third-party conformity assessment) | Article 6(1), full Title III application |
| **2 August 2029** | Commission conducts first evaluation and review of the Regulation | Article 111 |
| **Every 4 years after 2029** | Periodic review by the Commission | Article 111 |

### Phase 1: Entry into Force (1 August 2024)

The Regulation enters into force but most substantive obligations do not yet apply. This phase establishes the legal framework and begins the countdown to applicable dates. Member States and the Commission begin setting up governance structures.

### Phase 2: Prohibited Practices and AI Literacy (2 February 2025)

Six months after entry into force:

- **Article 5 prohibitions take effect**: All eight categories of banned AI systems become enforceable. Systems performing social scoring, subliminal manipulation, exploitation of vulnerabilities, predictive policing based solely on profiling, untargeted facial image scraping, workplace/education emotion recognition, biometric categorisation of sensitive attributes, and (with exceptions) real-time remote biometric identification in public spaces are now illegal.
- **Article 4 AI literacy applies**: Providers and deployers must ensure sufficient AI literacy among staff dealing with AI systems, taking into account their technical knowledge, experience, education, training, and the context in which the systems are used.
- **Maximum penalty for violations**: EUR 35 million or 7% of worldwide annual turnover.

### Phase 3: GPAI and Governance (2 August 2025)

Twelve months after entry into force:

- **GPAI model obligations (Articles 51-56)**: All GPAI model providers must maintain technical documentation, provide downstream information, comply with copyright law, and publish training content summaries. Systemic risk models (10^25 FLOPs threshold) must additionally perform model evaluation, adversarial testing, serious incident tracking, and cybersecurity.
- **AI Office (Article 57-58)**: Fully operational for GPAI oversight and enforcement.
- **European AI Board (Article 60)**: Advisory body operational.
- **Advisory Forum (Article 62)**: Stakeholder input body operational.
- **National competent authorities (Article 63)**: Member States must have designated notifying authorities and market surveillance authorities.
- **Codes of practice (Article 55)**: AI Office facilitates GPAI codes of practice.

### Phase 4: High-Risk Annex III and Transparency (2 August 2026)

Twenty-four months after entry into force. **This is the primary compliance deadline for aiact-mcp.**

- **Annex III high-risk systems**: Full suite of obligations applies to AI systems in the eight Annex III use-case areas (biometrics, critical infrastructure, education, employment, essential services, law enforcement, migration, justice/democracy).
  - Requirements: Articles 8-15 (risk management, data governance, technical documentation, logging, transparency, human oversight, accuracy/robustness)
  - Provider obligations: Articles 16-29 (QMS, documentation, conformity assessment, registration, corrective action)
  - Conformity assessment: Article 43, Annexes VI and VII
  - EU database registration: Article 49
  - CE marking: Article 48
- **Article 50 transparency obligations**: Chatbot disclosure, synthetic content marking, emotion recognition/biometric categorisation disclosure, deepfake disclosure.
- **Penalties**: Full three-tier penalty structure enforceable (Articles 99-101).
- **AI regulatory sandboxes (Article 57)**: Member States must establish at least one.
- **Fundamental rights impact assessment (Article 27)**: Public bodies deploying Annex III high-risk systems must complete before deployment.
- **Right to explanation (Article 80)**: Affected persons may request explanation of high-risk AI decisions with legal or significant effects.

### Phase 5: High-Risk Annex I Product Safety (2 August 2027)

Thirty-six months after entry into force:

- **Annex I high-risk systems**: AI systems that are safety components of products covered by EU harmonisation legislation listed in Annex I, which require third-party conformity assessment under that legislation, become subject to full high-risk obligations.
- This covers AI in machinery, medical devices, toys, lifts, radio equipment, motor vehicles, civil aviation, personal protective equipment, and other regulated products.
- The later deadline reflects the need to align with existing product safety conformity assessment procedures.

### Transitional Provisions (Article 110)

- AI systems already placed on the market or put into service before the applicable dates are not required to comply retroactively, **unless** they undergo a **substantial modification** after the applicable date.
- A substantial modification means a change that affects compliance or alters the intended purpose for which the system was assessed.
- Operators of systems already on the market should plan for compliance in advance of applicable dates, as any significant update after the deadline triggers full compliance requirements.

## Product Relevance
- **aiact-mcp**: The **2 August 2026** deadline is the product's primary compliance target. All Annex III high-risk AI system detection and compliance gap analysis must be operational before this date. The product should communicate urgency to users: organisations deploying AI systems in Annex III areas need conformity assessment, technical documentation, risk management systems, and EU database registration completed by this date. The earlier GPAI deadline (2 August 2025) is already past, meaning GPAI obligations are already enforceable when users interact with the product.
