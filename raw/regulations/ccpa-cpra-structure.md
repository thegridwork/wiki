# California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) + California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA)
## Cal. Civ. Code §§ 1798.100-1798.199.100
## CCPA effective January 1, 2020 | CPRA amendments effective January 1, 2023

### Consumer Rights

#### CCPA Original Rights
- **§1798.100**: Right to know what personal information is collected
- **§1798.105**: Right to delete personal information
- **§1798.110**: Right to know what categories/specific pieces collected
- **§1798.115**: Right to know about selling/sharing of personal information
- **§1798.120**: Right to opt-out of sale of personal information ("Do Not Sell My Personal Information")
- **§1798.125**: Non-discrimination for exercising rights

#### CPRA New/Enhanced Rights
- **§1798.100(d)**: Right to know about automated decision-making
- **§1798.106**: Right to correct inaccurate personal information
- **§1798.120(a)**: Right to opt-out of sharing (expanded from just sale)
- **§1798.121**: Right to limit use of sensitive personal information
- **§1798.135**: "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" link requirement
- **§1798.185(a)(16)**: Right to opt-out of automated decision-making (CPPA rulemaking)

### Business Obligations
- **§1798.100(b)**: Disclose categories collected, purposes, third parties
- **§1798.100(d)**: Provide information about automated decision-making
- **§1798.130**: Methods for submitting consumer requests
- **§1798.135(a)**: Clear and conspicuous "Do Not Sell or Share" link on homepage
- **§1798.135(b)**: Honor Global Privacy Control (GPC) signals
- **§1798.140(e)**: Definition of "business" (>$25M revenue, or >100K consumers, or >50% revenue from selling)

### Sensitive Personal Information (CPRA addition)
- §1798.121: Consumers can limit use to what's necessary
- Categories: SSN, financial account info, geolocation, racial/ethnic origin, religious beliefs, union membership, mail/email/text content, genetic data, biometric data, health data, sex life/sexual orientation

### Enforcement
- California Privacy Protection Agency (CPPA) — created by CPRA
- Attorney General enforcement
- Private right of action for data breaches (§1798.150)
- Fines: $2,500 per unintentional violation, $7,500 per intentional violation

### Key Differences from GDPR
- Opt-out model (vs. GDPR's opt-in consent)
- "Sale" defined broadly (includes sharing for cross-context behavioral advertising)
- No data processing agreements required (but service provider contracts needed)
- No DPO requirement
- GPC/Do Not Sell specific mechanism (no GDPR equivalent)

## US State Privacy Laws (other major ones)

### Virginia VCDPA (effective Jan 1, 2023)
- Consumer rights: access, correct, delete, portability, opt-out of targeted advertising/sale/profiling
- Threshold: 100K consumers or 25K consumers + 50% revenue from sale
- No private right of action

### Colorado CPA (effective July 1, 2023)
- Similar rights to VCDPA
- Universal opt-out mechanism required by July 1, 2024
- Threshold: 100K consumers or 25K consumers + sale revenue

### Connecticut CTDPA (effective July 1, 2023)
- Similar to VCDPA/CPA
- Recognizes Global Privacy Control

### Texas TDPSA (effective July 1, 2024)
- No revenue/volume threshold — applies to all businesses
- Similar rights structure

### Oregon OCPA (effective July 1, 2024)
- Includes non-profit organizations
- Right to list of third-party data recipients

### Montana, Tennessee, Indiana, Iowa, Delaware, New Hampshire, New Jersey, Nebraska, Maryland, Minnesota, Kentucky, Rhode Island — all enacted 2023-2025, staggered effective dates through 2026
