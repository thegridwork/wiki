# EU AI Act
## Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 — Entered into force August 1, 2024

### Timeline
- **February 2, 2025**: Prohibited AI practices (Title II) apply
- **August 2, 2025**: GPAI obligations (Chapter V) + governance provisions apply
- **August 2, 2026**: High-risk AI system obligations (Title III Chapter 2, Articles 16-49) apply
- **August 2, 2027**: Article 6(1) high-risk systems (Annex I) fully apply

### Title I — General Provisions (Articles 1-4)
- Article 1: Subject matter
- Article 2: Scope (excludes military, national security, research, personal non-professional use)
- Article 3: Definitions (AI system, provider, deployer, operator, biometric, emotion recognition, etc.)
- Article 4: AI literacy obligation

### Title II — Prohibited AI Practices (Article 5)
- Article 5(1)(a): Subliminal manipulation causing harm
- Article 5(1)(b): Exploitation of vulnerabilities (age, disability, social/economic)
- Article 5(1)(c): Social scoring by public authorities
- Article 5(1)(d): Real-time remote biometric identification in public spaces (law enforcement exceptions)
- Article 5(1)(e): Untargeted scraping for facial recognition databases
- Article 5(1)(f): Emotion recognition in workplace and education
- Article 5(1)(g): Biometric categorization for sensitive attributes (race, religion, sexual orientation)
- Article 5(1)(h): Predictive policing based solely on profiling

### Title III — High-Risk AI Systems

#### Chapter 1 — Classification (Articles 6-7)
- Article 6(1): AI as safety component of product covered by Annex I EU harmonisation legislation
- Article 6(2): AI in areas listed in Annex III
- Article 6(3): Exception if no significant risk to health, safety, or fundamental rights
- Article 7: Amendments to Annex III

#### Annex III — High-Risk Use Cases
1. Biometrics (identification, categorization, emotion recognition)
2. Critical infrastructure (energy, water, gas, heating, transport, digital)
3. Education and vocational training (admissions, assessment, proctoring)
4. Employment (recruitment, performance, task allocation, termination)
5. Essential services (credit scoring, insurance, emergency services)
6. Law enforcement (individual risk assessment, polygraphs, evidence evaluation)
7. Migration, asylum, border control (risk assessment, document verification)
8. Administration of justice (judicial research, sentencing)

#### Chapter 2 — Requirements for High-Risk Systems (Articles 8-15)
- Article 8: Compliance with requirements
- Article 9: Risk management system
- Article 10: Data and data governance
- Article 11: Technical documentation (per Annex IV)
- Article 12: Record-keeping (logging)
- Article 13: Transparency and information to deployers
- Article 14: Human oversight
- Article 15: Accuracy, robustness, cybersecurity

#### Chapter 3 — Obligations of Providers/Deployers (Articles 16-29)
- Article 16: Provider obligations
- Article 17: Quality management system
- Article 26: Deployer obligations
- Article 27: Fundamental rights impact assessment (deployers)

#### Chapter 4 — Notifying Bodies (Articles 30-39)
#### Chapter 5 — Standards, Conformity, Certificates (Articles 40-49)
- Article 43: Conformity assessment procedures
- Article 47: EU declaration of conformity
- Article 49: Registration in EU database

### Title IV — Transparency Obligations (Article 50)
- Article 50(1): AI systems interacting with persons → disclose AI nature
- Article 50(2): Synthetic content (deepfakes) → label as AI-generated
- Article 50(3): Emotion recognition / biometric categorization → inform subjects
- Article 50(4): Deep fakes → disclose artificial generation

### Title V — General-Purpose AI Models (Articles 51-56)
- Article 51: Classification of GPAI models
- Article 52: Obligations for all GPAI providers (technical documentation, copyright policy, content summary)
- Article 53: Systemic risk GPAI (>10^25 FLOPs training compute threshold)
  - Model evaluation, adversarial testing, incident tracking, cybersecurity
- Article 55: Codes of practice for GPAI
- Article 56: AI Office oversight of GPAI

### Title VI-XII — Governance, Enforcement, Penalties
- Article 71: Fines
  - Prohibited practices: up to €35M or 7% global turnover
  - High-risk non-compliance: up to €15M or 3% global turnover
  - Incorrect information: up to €7.5M or 1% global turnover
  - SME/startup: lower of percentage or fixed amount

### Annex IV — Technical Documentation Requirements
- General description, detailed development process, monitoring/functioning/control, risk management

### Key Implementing Acts & Standards (expected)
- Harmonised standards under Article 40
- Common specifications under Article 41
- Codes of practice for GPAI under Article 55
- Guidance on high-risk classification under Article 6(3)
