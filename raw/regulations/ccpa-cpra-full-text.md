# CCPA / CPRA Structured Reference

California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) — Cal. Civ. Code Title 1.81.5
California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA) — 2020 ballot proposition amending CCPA

**Effective dates:**
- CCPA: January 1, 2020
- CPRA amendments: January 1, 2023 (lookback to January 1, 2022)
- CPPA enforcement authority: July 1, 2023

---

## Key Thresholds (Applicability)

A for-profit entity doing business in California that meets ANY of:

| Threshold | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Annual gross revenue | > $25,000,000 |
| Data volume | Buys, sells, or shares personal information of >= 100,000 consumers or households per year (CPRA raised from 50,000) |
| Revenue from data | Derives >= 50% of annual revenue from selling or sharing consumers' personal information |

Exemptions: HIPAA-covered entities (health data), GLBA-covered entities (financial data), nonprofits, government agencies.

## Consumer Rights Detail

### 1. Right to Know (ss 1798.100, 1798.110, 1798.115)
- Request categories of PI collected, sold, or shared
- Request specific pieces of PI collected
- Business must provide information for the preceding 12 months (CPRA extends beyond 12 months if PI collected on or after January 1, 2022)
- Delivered free of charge, within 45 days (extendable to 90)

### 2. Right to Delete (s 1798.105)
- Request deletion of PI a business has collected
- Business must also direct service providers and contractors to delete
- Exceptions: complete a transaction, detect security incidents, exercise free speech, comply with legal obligation, internal uses reasonably aligned with consumer expectations

### 3. Right to Correct (s 1798.106) — CPRA addition
- Request correction of inaccurate PI
- Business must use commercially reasonable efforts to correct

### 4. Right to Opt-Out of Sale/Sharing (s 1798.120)
- "Sale" = exchanging PI for monetary or other valuable consideration
- "Sharing" (CPRA addition) = making PI available for cross-context behavioral advertising, regardless of monetary exchange
- Consumers 16+ must opt in; consumers under 16 require affirmative authorization (under 13: parent/guardian)

### 5. Right to Limit Use of Sensitive Personal Information (s 1798.121) — CPRA addition
- Consumer can restrict use to what is necessary to perform the service/provide the goods requested
- Must provide a "Limit the Use of My Sensitive Personal Information" link

### 6. Right to Non-Discrimination (s 1798.125)
- Cannot deny goods/services, charge different prices, provide different quality, or suggest different treatment
- Financial incentive programs permitted if reasonably related to PI value and consumer opts in

## Business Obligations

### Notice Requirements (s 1798.100, 1798.130)
- Privacy policy disclosing: categories of PI collected, purposes, categories of third parties, consumer rights
- Notice at or before collection
- Updated at least every 12 months

### Service Provider Requirements (s 1798.140(ag))
- Written contract required
- Must not sell or share PI received
- Must not retain, use, or disclose PI outside the direct business relationship
- Must comply with consumer deletion requests passed through from the business

### Contractor Requirements (s 1798.140(j)) — CPRA addition
- Same restrictions as service providers
- Must also allow the business to audit compliance
- Contractual certification that they understand and will comply with restrictions

### Data Minimization (CPRA)
- Collection and use of PI must be reasonably necessary and proportionate to the disclosed purposes
- Cannot retain PI longer than reasonably necessary for the disclosed purpose

## Sensitive Personal Information Categories (CPRA s 1798.140(ae))

| # | Category |
|---|----------|
| 1 | Social Security number, driver's license, state ID, passport number |
| 2 | Account log-in credentials (username + password, financial account + access code) |
| 3 | Precise geolocation (within 1,850 feet / ~563 meters) |
| 4 | Racial or ethnic origin |
| 5 | Religious or philosophical beliefs |
| 6 | Union membership |
| 7 | Contents of mail, email, and text messages (unless business is the intended recipient) |
| 8 | Genetic data |
| 9 | Biometric information for unique identification |
| 10 | Health information |
| 11 | Sex life or sexual orientation |

## CPPA Enforcement Powers (ss 1798.155, 1798.185, 1798.199)

- Independent state agency (not under AG office)
- Board: 5 members appointed by Governor, AG, Senate Rules Committee, and Speaker of the Assembly
- Powers:
  - Investigate possible violations
  - Issue subpoenas
  - Bring civil actions (administrative fines)
  - Adopt regulations with force of law
  - Conduct audits (cybersecurity audit authority via rulemaking)
  - Require risk assessments for high-risk processing
- Penalties:
  - $2,500 per unintentional violation
  - $7,500 per intentional violation
  - $7,500 per violation involving a minor under 16
- 30-day cure period: eliminated by CPRA for CPPA enforcement (AG cure period was in original CCPA)

## Private Right of Action (s 1798.150)

Limited to data breaches involving nonencrypted or nonredacted PI due to failure to implement reasonable security:
- Statutory damages: $100 to $750 per consumer per incident, OR actual damages (whichever is greater)
- Injunctive or declaratory relief
- Any other relief the court deems proper
- 30-day written notice required before filing (business may cure)

---

## Personal Information Definition (s 1798.140(v))

Information that identifies, relates to, describes, is reasonably capable of being associated with, or could reasonably be linked, directly or indirectly, with a particular consumer or household. Includes but is not limited to:

- Identifiers (name, alias, SSN, address, IP address, email, account name)
- Commercial information (purchase records, purchasing tendencies)
- Biometric information
- Internet/electronic network activity (browsing history, search history, interaction data)
- Geolocation data
- Audio, electronic, visual, thermal, olfactory information
- Professional or employment information
- Education information (non-FERPA)
- Inferences drawn to create a consumer profile

Excludes: publicly available government records, deidentified or aggregate data, HIPAA-covered health data, GLBA-covered financial data, FCRA-covered data.
